# An-Agent-Based-Model-to-Explore-the-Adoption-of-Travel-Plans-Among-SMEs

Car miles driven must fall by 9% before 2035 to reach carbon budget targets. Approximately, 24% of all car miles driven are commutes. Workplace travel plans (WTPs) can reduce car miles driven by up to 20% - they are fast (<2 years to implement), popular, cost-effective (<$50 per FTE). The speed and qualtiy of travel demand management must rapidly increase if the UK is to meet its carbon targets. Currently, WTPs can only be secured from large firms, not SMEs who employ 61% of the UK population (and therefore influence 61% of commutes). SMEs are already burdened by red tape, how could voluntary adoption be increased? This agent based model explores how social dynamics (incl. norms, mimetic isomorphism, and strategic complementarity) could lead to an s-shaped curve of adoption among smaller firms.

This repository tracks my development of my model. I begin with a toy model as I get re-acquainted with Python, and learn the workings of Mesa. I will then move to a full model after I have analysed my calibration data. Expect plenty of mistakes - I am not confident in Python, and I have never built an ABM before. I use Chat GPT to debug code and, in the beginning while I am learning Mesa, occasionally to write it. I take full accountability for any mistakes.
